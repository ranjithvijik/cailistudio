📚 UBalt AI Policy Framework Reference

The University of Baltimore recognizes that generative artificial intelligence (AI) technologies are transforming how students learn, create, and engage with knowledge. This policy framework establishes clear, consistent expectations for AI use across all courses while preserving faculty autonomy to determine appropriate integration levels based on course objectives and pedagogical goals.

Guiding Principles

  • Academic Integrity: Upholding honesty and original scholarship
  • Equitable Access: Ensuring all students have fair access to approved AI tools
  • Critical Thinking: Developing students' ability to evaluate and responsibly use AI
  • Transparency: Maintaining clear communication about expectations
  • Skill Development: Balancing AI assistance with essential learning outcomes

Distinguishing Standard Tools from Generative AI

Standard Digital Tools (Always Permitted):
Spellcheck, basic grammar checkers, thesauruses, search engines, library databases, and basic calculators.
These are not considered "AI use" under this policy and may be used at all times unless explicitly restricted.

Generative AI Tools (Subject to This Policy): "AI Use" refers specifically to generative AI tools capable of producing original content.

Category Examples
Text Generation ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Jasper
Image Generation DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion
Code Generation GitHub Copilot, Replit AI, Amazon CodeWhisperer
Audio/Video ElevenLabs, Synthesia, Runway
Research Assistants Elicit, Consensus, Perplexity AI
Writing Enhancement Grammarly Premium AI, QuillBot

Faculty Checklist

Before the semester begins, faculty should:

  • Select appropriate AI level(s) for the course
  • Include AI policy statement in syllabus
  • Specify permitted/prohibited tools
  • Define disclosure requirements
  • Explain consequences for violations
  • Identify support resources for students
  • Prepare to address student questions about AI use
  • Consider accessibility and equity implications
  • Plan for AI literacy instruction if appropriate

Academic Integrity & Violations

Violations: Unauthorized or undisclosed AI use constitutes a violation of academic integrity. Violations include:

  • Using AI when prohibited (Level 1 assignments)
  • Exceeding permitted AI use for the designated level
  • Failing to disclose AI use when required
  • Falsifying AI disclosure statements
  • Submitting AI-generated content as original work without authorization

Consequences: Faculty are required to select the appropriate consequences for violations in their syllabus. Options may include:

  • Assignment failure (zero grade)
  • Course failure
  • Academic probation
  • Notation on academic transcript
  • Suspension/expulsion for repeated or severe violations

Detection Methods: AI detection software, comparison with prior student work, oral examination/defense, review of required AI interaction logs, and analysis of writing style consistency.

Appeals Process: Students who believe they have been wrongly accused may appeal through the University's standard academic integrity appeals process. Documentation of AI use (or non-use) should be preserved and submitted as part of any appeal.
